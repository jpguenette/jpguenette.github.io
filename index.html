I am currently an internal medicine intern at Mount Auburn Hospital and a clinical fellow at Harvard University. Following my internship, in July 2014, I will be joining Brigham & Women's Hospital as a radiology resident.

My primary research goal is to minimize medical treatment morbidities, medical waste, and medical costs through the development and deployment of diagnostic and therapeutic imaging-related technologies that have the potential for large-scale application. Throughout medical school I conducted clinical research with Damian Dupuy, studying outcomes of and imaging features associated with percutaneous image-guided tumor ablation. I am also fascinated by (though not necessary well-versed in) human and computer vision, human morphology, and human evolutionary biology.

I earned my MD from Brown University and my BA from Dartmouth College. Prior to medical school I worked in New York City at Crystal & Company for over five years as a business analyst and completed a postbac at Columbia University.

I am on Twitter and LinkedIn and I am married to Margarita Martinez, an actor, TV host, and singer-songwriter.
